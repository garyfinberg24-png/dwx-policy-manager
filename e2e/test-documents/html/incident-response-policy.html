Incident Response Policy

Policy ID: FD-IS-004 | Version 5.1 | Effective: 1 March 2026

Critical Policy: All employees must report suspected security incidents immediately. Delays in reporting can significantly increase the impact of a breach. Contact the Security Operations Centre (SOC) at ext. 4400 or email security@firstdigital.com.

1. Purpose

This policy establishes the procedures for identifying, reporting, assessing, responding to, and recovering from information security incidents at First Digital. A structured incident response capability minimises the impact of security events, preserves evidence for investigation, and ensures compliance with mandatory breach notification requirements.

2. Scope

This policy covers all security events and incidents affecting First Digital's information systems, data, networks, physical premises, and personnel. It applies to all employees, contractors, and managed service providers.

5. Incident Response Phases

5.1 Preparation

The CSIRT maintains incident response playbooks, communication templates, and forensic toolkits. Annual tabletop exercises are conducted to test response readiness. All employees receive security awareness training that covers incident identification and reporting.

5.2 Identification and Reporting

  • Any employee who suspects a security incident must report it to the SOC immediately via phone (ext. 4400), email (security@firstdigital.com), or the ServiceNow incident portal.
  • The SOC performs initial triage within 15 minutes of receipt to confirm the incident and assign severity.
  • Automated alerts from SIEM, EDR, and DLP tools are triaged by the SOC 24/7.

5.3 Containment

  • Short-term containment: Isolate affected systems from the network to prevent lateral movement. Preserve volatile evidence (memory dumps, running processes).
  • Long-term containment: Apply temporary fixes to allow business operations to continue while the root cause is investigated.

5.4 Eradication and Recovery

Once contained, the CSIRT identifies and removes the root cause. Affected systems are rebuilt from known-good backups, patched, and hardened before being returned to production. Recovery is verified through vulnerability scanning and monitoring.

5.5 Post-Incident Review

A post-incident review (PIR) is conducted within 5 business days of incident closure. The PIR documents the timeline, root cause, impact assessment, response effectiveness, and improvement recommendations. PIR findings are presented to the Information Security Committee and tracked to completion.

6. Breach Notification

Where a data breach involves personal information, First Digital will comply with the Notifiable Data Breaches (NDB) scheme under the Privacy Act 1988. The Privacy Officer must be notified within 24 hours of a confirmed data breach to assess notification obligations. Notifications to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) and affected individuals will be made within the statutory timeframe.

7. Evidence Preservation

All evidence must be collected and preserved using forensically sound methods. A chain of custody log must be maintained for all evidence items. Original evidence must never be modified; analysis is performed on forensic copies only.
