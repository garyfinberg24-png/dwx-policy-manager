Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA) Compliance Framework

Version 3.0 · Effective 1 January 2026 · Policy Owner: Nomsa Dlamini, Chief Privacy Officer

Key Change in v3.0: Enhanced data breach notification timelines reduced from 72 hours to 48 hours. Cross-border transfer requirements updated to align with AU/GDPR adequacy framework.

1. Purpose and Scope

This policy establishes the framework for compliance with the Protection of Personal Information Act, 2013 (Act No. 4 of 2013) ("POPIA"). It applies to all employees, contractors, and third-party processors who handle personal information on behalf of the organisation.

The policy ensures that the organisation processes personal information in a lawful, reasonable, and responsible manner, protecting the privacy rights of data subjects as enshrined in Section 14 of the Constitution of the Republic of South Africa.

2. Key Definitions

  • Data Subject — The natural or juristic person to whom personal information relates
  • Responsible Party — The organisation that determines the purpose and means of processing
  • Operator — A third party processing data on behalf of the responsible party
  • Information Officer — The designated compliance officer (currently Sipho Nkosi)
  • Special Personal Information — Religious beliefs, race, trade union membership, health, biometric data

3. Processing Conditions

All processing of personal information must comply with the eight conditions for lawful processing:

3.1 Accountability

The responsible party must ensure that the conditions set out in POPIA are complied with at the time of determining the purpose and means of processing and during the processing itself.

3.2 Processing Limitation

Personal information must be processed lawfully and in a reasonable manner that does not infringe the privacy of the data subject. Processing is only permitted with consent, for contract performance, legal compliance, or legitimate interest.

⚠ Compliance Note: Employees who process personal information without a valid legal basis may face disciplinary action up to and including dismissal. Wilful or negligent non-compliance may also result in personal liability under Section 99 of POPIA.

3.3 Purpose Specification

Personal information must be collected for a specific, explicitly defined, and lawful purpose. Records of personal information must not be retained any longer than is necessary to achieve the purpose for which the information was collected.

3.4 Further Processing Limitation

Further processing of personal information must be compatible with the original purpose of collection. The Information Officer must approve any secondary use of personal information.

4. Data Breach Notification

In the event of a security compromise, the Information Officer must be notified within 24 hours of discovery. The Information Regulator and affected data subjects must be notified within 48 hours of confirmation.

5. Cross-Border Transfers

Personal information may only be transferred to a foreign country if:

  • The recipient country has adequate data protection legislation
  • The data subject has consented to the transfer
  • The transfer is necessary for contract performance
  • Binding corporate rules or approved contracts are in place
