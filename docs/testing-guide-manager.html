Role Overview & Navigation

ⓘ
Who is the Manager?
The Manager role (Tier 3) is for department managers and people managers responsible for overseeing policy compliance within their teams. Managers have all Employee and Author capabilities plus: policy approval/rejection, distribution campaign management, analytics access, delegation control, and the Settings cog for navigation preferences. Managers are the key decision-makers in the policy governance workflow.

Approve & Reject Policies

ⓘ
Approval Workflow Context:
When an Author submits a policy for review, it enters an approval chain. Chains can have multiple levels with different approval modes:
• Sequential — approvers act one at a time, in order
• Parallel — all approvers at a level act simultaneously; all must approve
• First Approver — first response at the level determines the outcome

The Manager is typically the first-level approver. If additional levels exist, the approval progresses automatically after the Manager's decision.
⚠
Pre-requisite: The Author tester should have submitted a policy for review (AUT-07) before this section. The Manager test account should be listed as a reviewer.
MGR-02 Approve a Policy P1 Critical
Test the complete approval workflow — receive notification, review the policy, and approve it.
  1. 1
    Check notifications for a review request
    The notification bell should show: "Policy submitted for your review: [Title]". Click the notification to navigate to the policy.
  2. 2
    Review the policy content
    Read through the policy on the PolicyDetails page. Verify all content and metadata are present.
  3. 3
    Click "Approve"
    An "Approve" button should be visible (since you are a reviewer and the policy is In Review). Click it. You may be prompted to add optional comments.
  4. 4
    Confirm the approval
    Confirm the approval in any dialog that appears.
  5. 5
    Verify the status change
    The policy status should change from "In Review" to "Approved". The Author should receive a notification: "Policy Approved: [Title]".
✓ Expected Results
  • Approval notification received from Author's submission
  • "Approve" button visible for In Review policies where you are a reviewer
  • Approval succeeds with confirmation message
  • Policy status changes to "Approved"
  • ApprovedDate set to current timestamp
  • Approval record created in PM_Approvals
  • History entry in PM_ApprovalHistory
  • Author notified of approval
MGR-03 Reject a Policy with Reason P1 Critical
Test the rejection workflow — provide feedback and send the policy back to the Author for revision.
⚠
Pre-requisite: Have the Author submit a second policy for review, or use a different In Review policy.
  1. 1
    Open a policy that is In Review
    Navigate to a policy assigned to you for review.
  2. 2
    Click "Reject"
    A "Reject" button should be visible alongside "Approve".
  3. 3
    Provide a rejection reason (required)
    A text field should appear requiring a reason. Enter meaningful feedback, e.g., "Section 3 needs more detail on data handling procedures." This field is mandatory — the rejection should not proceed without a reason.
  4. 4
    Confirm the rejection
    Submit the rejection.
  5. 5
    Verify the outcome
    The policy should return to "Draft" status. The rejection reason should be stored and visible to the Author.
✓ Expected Results
  • Rejection requires a mandatory reason — cannot submit without it
  • Policy returns to "Draft" status
  • RejectionReason and RejectedDate stored
  • Author receives rejection notification with the reason
  • Policy is editable again by the Author

Approval Delegation

ⓘ
What is Delegation?
Delegation allows a manager to temporarily assign their approval responsibilities to another person (e.g., during holidays or leave). There are two types:
• Automatic Delegation — configure a date range and delegate; all new approvals route to the delegate automatically
• Manual Delegation — delegate a single specific approval to another person
MGR-04 Create an Approval Delegation P2 High
Test creating an automatic delegation rule that redirects approval requests to a delegate during a specified date range.
  1. 1
    Navigate to the Manager Dashboard or Author Dashboard → Delegations tab
    Go to PolicyManagerView.aspx or PolicyAuthor.aspx and click the "Delegations" tab.
  2. 2
    Click "Create Delegation"
    A form should appear with fields for delegate selection and date range.
  3. 3
    Fill in the delegation form
    • Delegate To — use the people picker to select another manager
    • Start Date — today or a future date
    • End Date — a date after the start date
    • Process Types — which types of approvals to delegate (if configurable)
  4. 4
    Save the delegation
    Click "Save" or "Create". The delegation should appear in the delegations list as "Active" (if current date is within the range) or "Scheduled".
  5. 5
    Verify delegation takes effect
    Have the Author tester submit a new policy for review. If the delegation is active, the approval request should route to the delegate instead of you. The delegate should receive the notification.
✓ Expected Results
  • Delegation form allows selecting a delegate and date range
  • Delegation saved and appears in the list
  • When active, new approvals route to the delegate
  • Original approver stored as OriginalApproverId
  • Both delegate and original approver notified
  • Record created in PM_ApprovalDelegations

Publish a Policy

⚠
Critical Workflow: Publishing is the most impactful action in the system. It triggers: version creation, distribution record generation, acknowledgement records for all target users, notification emails, and reminder scheduling. This is a non-reversible action — once published, the version is permanently recorded.
MGR-05 Publish an Approved Policy P1 Critical
Test the full publish workflow — from approved policy to live distribution with acknowledgement generation.
⚠
Pre-requisite: A policy must be in "Approved" status (completed in MGR-02).
  1. 1
    Open the approved policy
    Navigate to the policy approved in MGR-02. Its status should show "Approved".
  2. 2
    Click "Publish"
    A "Publish" button should be available for Approved policies. Click it.
  3. 3
    Configure publication settings (if prompted)
    You may be asked to confirm or set:
    • Effective Date — when the policy takes effect (default: today)
    • Distribution Scope — All Employees, Department, Location, Role, Custom, New Hires Only
    • Read Timeframe — how long employees have to acknowledge
  4. 4
    Confirm the publication
    Review the target user count and confirm. This triggers the full publish cascade.
  5. 5
    Verify the results
    After publication, check:
    • Policy status changes to "Published"
    • Version bumps to 1.0 (or next major)
    • IsActive = true
    • PublishedDate set
    • Target employees receive "New Policy Published" notifications
  6. 6
    Verify from the Employee perspective
    Log in as the Employee test account. The published policy should appear in "My Policies" with an "Unread" status and the correct due date.
✓ Expected Results
  • Policy status: Approved → Published
  • Version: 1.0 (new major version created)
  • Version record in PM_PolicyVersions
  • Distribution record in PM_PolicyDistributions
  • Acknowledgement records in PM_PolicyAcknowledgements (one per target user)
  • Reminder schedules created (3-day, 1-day, overdue)
  • Notifications sent to target users
  • Policy visible in Employee's My Policies dashboard

Distribution Campaigns

🚀
Navigate to: PolicyDistribution.aspx
The Distribution page allows managers to create targeted distribution campaigns — sending specific policies or policy packs to defined groups of employees. This is separate from the automatic distribution that happens when a policy is published.
MGR-06 Create a Distribution Campaign P1 Critical
Test creating a new distribution campaign to push policies to targeted employees.
  1. 1
    Open the Distribution page
    Click "Distribution" in the nav bar. The page should load showing existing campaigns (or empty state) and a "Create Campaign" button.
  2. 2
    Click "Create Campaign"
    A form or dialog should appear for defining the campaign.
  3. 3
    Fill in campaign details
    • Campaign Name — e.g., "Q1 2026 Security Policy Update"
    • Policy / Policy Pack — select from dropdown (should show published policies and packs)
    • Distribution Scope — target audience selection
    • Scheduled Date — when to send
    • Due Date — acknowledgement deadline
  4. 4
    Save the campaign
    Click "Create" or "Save". The campaign should appear in the campaigns list.
  5. 5
    Verify the campaign card
    The campaign card should show: name, policy/pack, target count, status (Active/Scheduled), and creation date.
✓ Expected Results
  • Campaign form populates policy and pack dropdowns from live data
  • Campaign saves and appears in the list
  • Record created in PM_PolicyDistributions
  • Campaign card displays all metadata accurately
MGR-07 Monitor Campaign Metrics P2 High
Verify the campaign detail view shows real-time acknowledgement metrics and recipient tracking.
  1. 1
    Click on an active campaign
    Select a campaign that has been distributed. The detail view should open.
  2. 2
    Verify campaign metrics
    The metrics section should display:
    • Total Sent — number of acknowledgement requests sent
    • Delivered — successfully delivered
    • Opened — recipients who opened the policy
    • Acknowledged — completed acknowledgements
    • Overdue — past due date without acknowledgement
    • Acknowledgement Rate — percentage complete
  3. 3
    Check the recipient list
    If available, view the list of recipients with their individual status (Sent, Opened, Acknowledged, Overdue).
✓ Expected Results
  • Metrics display with accurate real-time values
  • Acknowledgement rate calculates correctly (Acknowledged / Sent × 100)
  • Recipient list shows individual statuses
  • Metrics update after employees acknowledge the policy

Analytics Dashboard

🚀
Navigate to: PolicyAnalytics.aspx
The Analytics Dashboard provides executive-level insights across 6 tabs. It pulls live data from multiple SharePoint lists (PM_Policies, PM_PolicyAcknowledgements, PM_PolicyAuditLog, PM_PolicyQuizzes, PM_PolicyQuizResults) and computes KPIs, trends, and compliance metrics.
MGR-08 Analytics — Executive Dashboard Tab P1 Critical
Verify the Executive tab loads with live KPI data from SharePoint lists.
  1. 1
    Open the Analytics page
    Click "Analytics" in the nav bar. The dashboard should load with a brief loading state, then display the Executive tab by default.
  2. 2
    Verify Executive KPI cards
    The Executive tab should display summary cards such as:
    • Total Policies — count of policies in PM_Policies
    • Published Policies — count with status Published
    • Overall Compliance Rate — percentage of acknowledged/total
    • Pending Acknowledgements — count awaiting action
    • Active Quizzes — count from PM_PolicyQuizzes
  3. 3
    Verify data is live (not mock)
    Cross-reference the "Total Policies" count with the actual number of policies in the SharePoint list. They should match. If the lists are empty, the dashboard should show zeros (not mock data).
✓ Expected Results
  • Dashboard loads without errors
  • KPI cards display numeric values (not "undefined" or "NaN")
  • Values match live SharePoint list data
  • If SP lists are empty/unprovisioned, fallback mock data displays gracefully
MGR-09 Analytics — All 6 Tabs P2 High
Verify all 6 analytics tabs load and display content.
  1. 1
    Click through each tab
    Switch between all 6 tabs and verify each loads:
    • Executive — KPI summary cards and overview charts
    • Policy Metrics — per-policy statistics, status breakdown
    • Acknowledgements — acknowledgement rates, compliance trends
    • SLA — SLA compliance, target days vs actuals, warning thresholds
    • Compliance — risk-level breakdown, department compliance
    • Audit — audit log entries, event timeline
  2. 2
    Verify no tab shows errors or blank content
    Each tab should display charts, tables, or cards with data. If no data exists, a friendly "No data available" message should appear instead of errors.
✓ Expected Results
  • All 6 tabs switch correctly without errors
  • Each tab renders its specific content type (cards, charts, tables)
  • No JavaScript console errors when switching tabs
  • Empty data states show friendly messages

Manager Dashboard

🚀
Navigate to: PolicyManagerView.aspx
The Manager Dashboard is the manager's command centre for team compliance oversight. It provides tabs for team compliance status, pending approvals, delegations, reviews, and reports.
MGR-10 Manager Dashboard — Team Compliance Tab P2 High
Verify the team compliance overview shows accurate compliance status for the manager's direct reports.
  1. 1
    Open the Manager View page
    Click "Policy Manager" in the nav bar.
  2. 2
    Verify the Team Compliance tab
    This should show a list of team members with their individual compliance percentages, overdue counts, and pending items. A summary card at the top should show the overall team compliance rate.
  3. 3
    Check other tabs
    Switch through the available tabs:
    • Approvals — pending approval requests
    • Delegations — active and past delegations
    • Reviews — policy review schedule
    • Reports — downloadable compliance reports
✓ Expected Results
  • Team members listed with compliance percentages
  • Overall team compliance rate displayed
  • All tabs load without errors
  • Approvals tab shows pending items (if any)
MGR-11 Send Escalation for Overdue Acknowledgements P2 High
Test the ability to escalate overdue acknowledgements by sending reminder notifications to non-compliant team members.
  1. 1
    Identify overdue team members
    In the Team Compliance tab or Distribution campaign detail, look for employees with overdue acknowledgements (status shown in red).
  2. 2
    Trigger escalation notifications
    If an "Escalate" or "Send Reminder" button exists, click it for one or more overdue employees.
  3. 3
    Verify escalation was sent
    A success message should confirm the escalation notification was queued. The notification should appear in the target employee's notification bell.
✓ Expected Results
  • Overdue employees identifiable in the dashboard
  • Escalation action available for overdue items
  • Notification queued in PM_NotificationQueue with Priority: High
  • Target employee receives escalation notification
