1. Purpose

This policy outlines First Digital's approach to performance management, ensuring all employees receive regular, constructive feedback and have clear development pathways aligned with business objectives.

2. Performance Review Cycle

January — Goal Setting

Employees and managers collaboratively set 3-5 SMART objectives for the year, aligned to team and company goals.

April — Q1 Check-in

Informal 30-minute conversation reviewing progress, removing blockers, and adjusting priorities as needed.

July — Mid-Year Review

Formal review with written documentation. Self-assessment submitted prior to meeting. Objectives may be recalibrated.

October — Q3 Check-in

Informal check-in focusing on year-end delivery and development plan progress.

December — Annual Review

Comprehensive performance assessment with rating, development plan for next year, and career conversation.

3. Performance Ratings

  • Exceptional (5) — Consistently exceeds all objectives with outstanding impact
  • Exceeds Expectations (4) — Regularly delivers above expected standards
  • Meets Expectations (3) — Consistently achieves all objectives to the required standard
  • Developing (2) — Partially meeting objectives; improvement plan required
  • Below Expectations (1) — Significant performance gaps; formal improvement plan initiated

4. Self-Assessment

Prior to both the mid-year and annual reviews, employees must complete a self-assessment covering progress against objectives, key achievements, challenges encountered, and development needs.

5. 360-Degree Feedback

For roles at Senior Manager level and above, 360-degree feedback from peers, direct reports, and cross-functional stakeholders will be incorporated into the annual review process.

6. Performance Improvement Plans (PIP)

Where an employee receives a rating of 2 or below, a formal Performance Improvement Plan will be initiated, specifying clear objectives, support measures, and a 90-day review timeline.

7. Development Planning

Every employee will have a Personal Development Plan (PDP) reviewed annually, covering technical skills, leadership capabilities, and career aspirations. First Digital provides an annual learning budget of $2,000 per employee.

8. Calibration

Following annual reviews, leadership teams conduct calibration sessions to ensure consistency and fairness of ratings across departments. HR facilitates these sessions and monitors for potential bias.

9. Record Keeping

All performance review documentation is stored in the HR system and accessible to the employee, their manager, and HR. Records are retained for the duration of employment plus seven years.
