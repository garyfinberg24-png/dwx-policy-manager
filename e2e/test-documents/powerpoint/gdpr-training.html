Understanding your obligations under the General Data Protection Regulation and the Australian Privacy Act when handling personal data.

Why this matters: GDPR penalties can reach up to 4% of annual global turnover or EUR 20 million, whichever is higher. Every employee has a role to play in protecting personal data.

The Seven Principles of GDPR

1. Lawfulness, Fairness & Transparency
Process data legally and be open about how you use it
2. Purpose Limitation
Collect data only for specified, explicit purposes
3. Data Minimisation
Only collect what you actually need
4. Accuracy
Keep personal data accurate and up to date
5. Storage Limitation
Do not keep data longer than necessary
6. Integrity & Confidentiality
Protect data against unauthorised access and loss
7. Accountability
You must be able to demonstrate compliance with all principles

Data Subject Rights

Individuals have these rights over their personal data. We must respond within 30 days.

  • Right to be Informed — Tell people how you use their data
  • Right of Access — People can request copies of their data
  • Right to Rectification — Correct inaccurate data when asked
  • Right to Erasure — Delete data when there is no legitimate reason to keep it
  • Right to Restrict Processing — Limit how data is used
  • Right to Data Portability — Provide data in a machine-readable format
  • Right to Object — People can object to certain types of processing
  • Rights related to Automated Decision Making — Right to human review of automated decisions

What You Must Do

  • Only access personal data you need for your role
  • Never share personal data with unauthorised parties
  • Use BCC when emailing multiple external recipients
  • Double-check email recipients before sending data
  • Store personal data in approved systems only (SharePoint, Workday)
  • Report any data breach or near-miss to the DPO within 24 hours
  • Forward any data subject access requests to privacy@firstdigital.com immediately
  • Dispose of physical documents containing personal data via secure shredding
Data Breach: A data breach must be reported to the relevant authority within 72 hours. Contact the Data Protection Officer immediately at dpo@firstdigital.com or Ext. 4500.

Scenarios — What Would You Do?

Scenario 1: A client requests a copy of all data you hold about them. What do you do?
Answer: Forward the request to privacy@firstdigital.com. Do not attempt to compile the data yourself.
Scenario 2: You accidentally email a spreadsheet with employee data to the wrong person. What do you do?
Answer: Immediately contact the DPO and your manager. Ask the recipient to delete the email without reading it.
Scenario 3: A colleague asks you to share a client contact list for a marketing campaign. What do you do?
Answer: Check if the contacts consented to marketing. Consult with Privacy team before sharing.

Summary

Your data protection responsibilities

  1. Only collect and access personal data you genuinely need
  2. Keep data accurate, secure, and only for as long as required
  3. Report breaches and access requests immediately
  4. Use approved systems and follow the Data Classification Policy
  5. When in doubt, ask the Data Protection Officer
