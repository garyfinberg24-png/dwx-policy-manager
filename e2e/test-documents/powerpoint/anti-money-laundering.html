The Three Stages of Money Laundering

Placement

Illegally obtained money is introduced into the financial system through deposits, purchases, or currency exchange.

Layering

Complex transactions disguise the money trail — transfers between accounts, shell companies, and cross-border movement.

Integration

The laundered money re-enters the legitimate economy through property purchases, business investments, or luxury goods.

Red Flags — What to Watch For

  • Client reluctant to provide identification or verification documents
  • Transactions that appear unnecessarily complex or lack business rationale
  • Requests to split transactions to avoid reporting thresholds
  • Payments from third parties unrelated to the transaction
  • Clients using multiple accounts or entities for no clear purpose
  • Unusually large cash payments or requests for cash refunds
  • Clients from high-risk jurisdictions with no clear business connection
  • Rapid movement of funds through accounts with minimal activity
  • Client's business activities inconsistent with their stated profile
  • Pressure to complete transactions quickly without proper due diligence

Your Obligations

Know Your Customer (KYC)

  • Verify client identity using approved documentation before onboarding
  • Conduct enhanced due diligence for high-risk clients (PEPs, sanctioned countries)
  • Keep client records up to date and review periodically

Report Suspicious Activity

  • Report internally to the Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO) immediately
  • Do NOT alert the client that a report has been made ("tipping off" is a criminal offence)
  • Document your concerns clearly with dates, amounts, and parties involved

Key Takeaways

Protecting First Digital from financial crime

  1. Always verify client identity before engaging in business
  2. Watch for transactions that seem unusual or lack business sense
  3. Report suspicions to the MLRO immediately and confidentially
  4. Never tip off a client about a suspicious activity report
  5. Keep thorough records of all client interactions and transactions
