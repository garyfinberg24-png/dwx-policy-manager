Activation Triggers

The BCP is activated when an event significantly disrupts normal operations:

  • Natural disaster — Fire, flood, earthquake, severe storm affecting office access
  • Pandemic — Widespread illness affecting workforce availability
  • Cyber incident — Ransomware, data breach, or sustained DDoS attack
  • Infrastructure failure — Power outage, internet outage, or building damage
  • Supply chain disruption — Critical vendor failure or service withdrawal
  • Civil disruption — Transport strikes, civil unrest, or government-imposed restrictions

Activation authority: CEO, COO, or CTO. Notification via mass SMS and Teams broadcast.

Your Role During a Disruption

  • Check your phone for SMS alerts from the Crisis Management Team
  • Log in to Teams from home — your manager will post instructions in your team channel
  • Follow the work-from-home procedures in the Remote Working Policy
  • Prioritise client-facing deliverables and critical deadlines
  • Report your status via the Employee Check-In form (link in SMS)
  • Do not speak to media — all media enquiries go to Communications
  • Attend daily stand-up briefings at the scheduled time until normal operations resume

Testing & Maintenance

A plan is only as good as its last test

  • Tabletop exercises: Quarterly with leadership team
  • Functional exercises: Semi-annual simulation of specific scenarios
  • Full-scale drill: Annual company-wide BCP activation test
  • DR failover test: Quarterly automated failover verification
  • Plan review: Updated after every exercise, incident, or significant change
