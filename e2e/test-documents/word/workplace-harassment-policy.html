1. Purpose

First Digital is committed to providing a safe, respectful, and inclusive working environment free from harassment, bullying, and discrimination. This policy outlines unacceptable behaviours, reporting mechanisms, and the consequences of policy violations.

2. Scope

This policy applies to all First Digital employees, contractors, interns, and visitors across all locations, including remote work environments and company-sponsored events.

3. Definitions

3.1 Harassment

Unwelcome conduct based on protected characteristics including race, gender, age, disability, religion, sexual orientation, or any other legally protected status that creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment.

3.2 Bullying

Repeated, unreasonable behaviour directed towards an employee or group of employees that creates a risk to health and safety, including verbal abuse, intimidation, and exclusion from work-related activities.

3.3 Sexual Harassment

Any unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favours, or other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature that affects employment decisions or creates a hostile work environment.

4. Prohibited Behaviours

The following behaviours are strictly prohibited:

  • Verbal abuse, threats, or intimidation
  • Offensive jokes, slurs, or name-calling based on protected characteristics
  • Unwelcome physical contact or gestures
  • Display of offensive materials in the workplace or on company systems
  • Cyberbullying via email, messaging, or social media
  • Deliberate exclusion or isolation of colleagues
  • Spreading malicious rumours or gossip
  • Retaliation against anyone who reports harassment

5. Reporting Procedures

Employees who experience or witness harassment or bullying should report the matter through one or more of the following channels:

  • Direct Manager — Raise the issue with your immediate supervisor
  • Human Resources — Contact HR at hr@firstdigital.com
  • Ethics Hotline — Anonymous reporting via the company ethics hotline
  • Policy Manager Portal — Submit a formal complaint through the DWx system

6. Investigation Process

All complaints will be treated seriously and investigated promptly. The investigation will typically follow these steps:

  • Acknowledgement of complaint within 24 hours
  • Appointment of an independent investigator
  • Interviews with all relevant parties
  • Review of evidence and documentation
  • Findings report within 15 business days
  • Communication of outcome to all parties

7. Consequences

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment, and may also result in legal proceedings where applicable.

8. Support Resources

First Digital provides access to confidential counselling through the Employee Assistance Programme (EAP). Contact details are available on the company intranet and through the DWx Policy Manager portal.

9. Review

This policy will be reviewed annually or whenever legislative changes require updates. All employees must acknowledge this policy upon hire and following any material revisions.
