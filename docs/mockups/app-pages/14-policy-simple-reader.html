Bring Your Own Device (BYOD) Policy

Purpose: This policy establishes guidelines for employees who use personal devices (smartphones, tablets, laptops) to access company resources, systems, and data.

1. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and temporary workers who use personally owned devices to access company email, documents, applications, or internal networks.

2. Device Registration

All personal devices used for work purposes must be registered with the IT department before accessing any company resources. Registration includes:

  • Device make, model, and operating system version
  • Employee acknowledgement of BYOD terms
  • Installation of required Mobile Device Management (MDM) software
  • Verification of minimum security requirements (encryption, screen lock)

3. Security Requirements

Registered devices must comply with the following minimum security standards at all times:

RequirementStandardEnforced By
Screen LockPIN (6+ digits) or biometricMDM Policy
EncryptionFull device encryption enabledMDM Policy
OS UpdatesWithin 30 days of releaseMDM Compliance Check
AntivirusActive and up to dateSelf-attestation
Jailbreak/RootNot permittedMDM Detection

4. Data Protection

Company data accessed on personal devices is subject to the same data protection requirements as data on company-owned devices:

  • Company data must not be stored locally on personal devices outside of approved applications
  • Cloud backup of company data to personal accounts is prohibited
  • Screenshots or recordings of confidential information are restricted
Important: Lost or stolen devices that have accessed company resources must be reported to IT within 24 hours. IT reserves the right to remotely wipe company data from registered devices.

5. Acceptable Use

Employees using personal devices for work acknowledge that:

  • Work-related communications may be subject to legal discovery
  • IT may monitor work-related applications and data on the device
  • The company is not responsible for personal data loss during a remote wipe
  • Devices must not be used in a manner that violates the IT Acceptable Use Policy

6. Support

IT support for personal devices is limited to:

  • MDM software installation and configuration
  • Company email and application setup
  • VPN configuration

IT does not provide support for personal applications, hardware issues, or operating system problems on personal devices.

7. Non-Compliance

Failure to comply with this policy may result in revocation of BYOD privileges, disciplinary action, and in cases involving data breaches, legal proceedings in accordance with applicable laws including POPIA.
