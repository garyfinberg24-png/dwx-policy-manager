Fire Safety & Evacuation Training

First Digital — Mandatory WHS Training 2026

Know your role, know your exits, know the plan. This training covers fire prevention, extinguisher use, and evacuation procedures for all First Digital offices.

Emergency Number: Dial 000 (or 112 from mobile) for Fire, Ambulance, or Police. Internal emergency extension: 9999.

Fire Prevention

  • Keep fire exits, corridors, and stairwells clear at all times
  • Do not overload power boards or daisy-chain extension leads
  • Report any damaged electrical equipment immediately
  • Store flammable materials in designated cabinets only
  • Never prop open fire doors
  • Switch off non-essential equipment at the end of the day
  • Know the location of fire extinguishers and blankets on your floor
  • Keep kitchen appliances clean and attend to cooking at all times

Using a Fire Extinguisher — PASS Method

Only attempt to fight a fire if it is small, contained, and you have a clear escape route behind you.

P
PULL the pin
A
AIM at the base of the fire
S

S
SWEEP side to side
Important: If the fire is larger than a rubbish bin, spreading rapidly, or producing toxic smoke — do NOT attempt to fight it. Evacuate immediately and call 000.

Evacuation Procedure

1Alarm Sounds

Stop work immediately. Do not collect personal belongings.

2Follow Wardens

Floor Wardens wear orange vests. Follow their instructions.

3Use Stairs

Never use lifts during evacuation. Assist anyone who needs help.

4Assembly Point

Proceed to your designated assembly point. Sydney: Martin Place.

5Roll Call

Report to your Floor Warden for headcount. Stay at assembly point.

6All Clear

Return to the building only when the Chief Warden gives the all-clear.

Key Takeaways

Fire safety is everyone's responsibility

  1. Know your two nearest fire exits
  2. Know the location of fire extinguishers on your floor
  3. Know your Floor Warden
  4. Know your assembly point
  5. Never ignore an alarm — treat every alarm as real
