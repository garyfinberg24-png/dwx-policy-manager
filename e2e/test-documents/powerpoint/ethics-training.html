Conflicts of Interest

A conflict of interest arises when your personal interests could influence — or appear to influence — your professional judgement.

Examples

  • Having a financial interest in a vendor or competitor
  • Hiring or supervising a family member or close friend
  • Accepting a second job that conflicts with your First Digital role
  • Using company resources for personal projects
  • Making business decisions that benefit you personally
Disclosure requirement: All actual, potential, or perceived conflicts must be declared to your manager and HR using the Conflict of Interest Declaration Form. Declarations are reviewed annually.

Gifts & Hospitality

Acceptable: A client sends a box of chocolates at Christmas (value under $100). Log it in the Gifts Register but you may accept.
Requires approval: A vendor invites you to a sporting event with a ticket value of $250. Must be pre-approved by your VP and logged in the Gifts Register.
Not acceptable: A prospective supplier offers you a weekend away. This must be declined regardless of value, as it could influence procurement decisions.

Rules

  • Gifts under $100 may be accepted and must be logged
  • Gifts $100-$500 require VP approval before acceptance
  • Gifts over $500 must be declined
  • Cash or cash equivalents (gift cards) must always be declined
  • Government officials: zero tolerance — no gifts of any value

Speaking Up

Your voice matters — and you are protected

If you witness or suspect unethical behaviour, fraud, corruption, or any violation of our Code of Conduct, you have a duty and a right to report it.

Reporting Channels

  • Your Manager — First point of contact for most concerns
  • HR Department — hr@firstdigital.com
  • General Counsel — For legal or regulatory matters
  • Ethics Hotline — Anonymous, 24/7, operated by independent third party

Whistleblower Protection: First Digital will not tolerate any retaliation against employees who report concerns in good faith. All reports are investigated confidentially and thoroughly.
