1. Purpose

This policy establishes the framework for managing employee leave entitlements at First Digital, ensuring compliance with employment legislation and promoting employee wellbeing through adequate rest and recovery time.

2. Scope

This policy applies to all permanent full-time and part-time employees. Contractors and casual employees should refer to their individual agreements for leave provisions.

4. Annual Leave

4.1 Booking Procedure

All annual leave requests must be submitted through the company HR system at least two weeks in advance. Managers should respond to requests within three business days.

4.2 Peak Period Restrictions

During designated peak periods (December 15 - January 5, and project-specific blackout dates), leave requests are subject to business need and may be declined. Requests for these periods should be submitted at least six weeks in advance.

4.3 Leave Loading

Annual leave attracts a 17.5% loading paid at the time the leave is taken, in accordance with applicable employment agreements.

5. Sick Leave

Employees must notify their manager as early as possible on the first day of absence. A medical certificate is required for absences exceeding two consecutive days or where a pattern of absence is identified.

Note: Unused sick leave does not attract a cash-out benefit upon termination but is maintained for the duration of employment.

6. Parental Leave

First Digital supports new parents with industry-leading parental leave provisions. Primary carers receive 16 weeks at full pay, while secondary carers receive 4 weeks. Employees must have completed 12 months of continuous service to be eligible.

7. Leave Without Pay

Leave without pay may be granted at management discretion for periods up to 12 months. Requests must be submitted in writing with a minimum of four weeks notice, outlining the reason and duration requested.

8. Public Holidays

Employees are entitled to all gazetted public holidays applicable to their state or territory. Where a public holiday falls on a non-working day, a substitute day will be arranged in consultation with the employee.

9. Responsibilities

  • Employees: Submit leave requests in advance, provide required documentation, ensure adequate handover
  • Managers: Approve/decline requests promptly, ensure team coverage, encourage leave usage
  • HR: Administer leave records, ensure compliance, report on leave patterns
